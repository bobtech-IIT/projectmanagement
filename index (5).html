Chapter 1: Introduction to PMI and the PMP Exam

What is PMI?

The Project Management Institute (PMI) is the leading global organization dedicated to advancing project management principles and practices. It sets standards, offers certifications, and provides resources that support project managers worldwide.

PMI Hierarchy

                PMI -> The Project Management Institute
                  |
                  ├── PMBOK Guide (A Guide to the Project Management Body of Knowledge)
                  |
                  ├── Certifications (PMP, CAPM, etc.)
                  |
                  ├── Standards (e.g., Agile Practice Guide)
                  |
                  └── Resources (Templates, Communities, etc.)

Overview of the PMP Exam

The Project Management Professional (PMP) credential is the most recognized certification for project managers, validating your knowledge and experience in leading projects effectively across various industries.

Exam Format and Scoring

  • Number of Questions: 180 (5 are pretest questions that do not affect your score)
  • Duration: 230 minutes
  • Question Type: Multiple-choice, multiple responses, matching, hotspot, and fill-in-the-blank
  • Passing Score: PMI does not publicly disclose the exact passing score.

Eligibility and Application Process

To be eligible for the PMP exam, you need to meet education and experience requirements and then apply through the PMI website.

  • Education: Secondary degree (high school diploma, associate degree) requires 5 years/7,500 hours leading projects + 35 hours of project management education
  • OR Four-year degree requires 3 years/4,500 hours leading projects + 35 hours of project management education

Chapter 2: Project Management Framework

Project, Program, and Portfolio Management

  • Project: A temporary endeavor undertaken to create a unique product, service, or result.
  • Program: A group of related projects managed in a coordinated manner to obtain benefits not available from managing them individually.
  • Portfolio: Collection of projects, programs, and operations managed as a group to achieve strategic objectives.

Chapter 3: The Five Process Groups

PMI organizes project management processes into five Process Groups. These groups are not phases but categories of processes that occur throughout the project life cycle.

The Five Process Groups Cycle (Text Flow)

Initiating --> Planning --> Executing --> Monitoring & Controlling --> Closing
  ↑                                                                 ↓
  └-------------------------------------------------------┘
  (Continuous Feedback and Iteration)
Process Group Description Typical Activities
Initiating Define a new project or phase by obtaining authorization. Develop Project Charter, Identify Stakeholders
Planning Establish the scope, objectives, and course of action. Define Scope, Schedule, Cost, Risk Plans
Executing Complete the work defined by the plan. Manage Teams, Resources, Quality
Monitoring and Controlling Track, review, and regulate the progress and performance. Performance Reports, Change Control
Closing Finalize all activities and formally close the project. Obtain Acceptance, Archive Documents

Chapter 5: Key Tools, Techniques, and Formulas

This chapter highlights important formulas and techniques used in project management.

Critical Path Method (CPM)

CPM is a project management technique used to determine the longest sequence of activities that must be completed on time for the project to be completed on time.

CPM Example (Simplified)

Activity  | Duration | Predecessors
----------|----------|---------------
A         | 2 days   | -
B         | 3 days   | A
C         | 4 days   | A
D         | 2 days   | B, C

Critical Path: A -> C -> D (Total duration = 8 days)

Earned Value Management (EVM)

EVM is a technique used to measure project performance against the baseline.

Earned Value Management (EVM) Formulas

PV = Planned Value
EV = Earned Value
AC = Actual Cost
CV = Cost Variance = EV - AC
SV = Schedule Variance = EV - PV
CPI = Cost Performance Index = EV / AC
SPI = Schedule Performance Index = EV / PV
ETC = Estimate To Complete = (BAC - EV) / CPI
EAC = Estimate At Completion = AC + ETC
BAC = Budget at Completion

Chapter 7: Exam Strategies and Best Practices

Time Management During Exam

Effective time management can help you finish the exam and have time for review.

What is the best way to manage time during the exam?

Allocate 1 minute per question, mark difficult questions, and use the remaining time to review marked questions.
The 80-80-70 rule suggests dividing your exam time as follows: - Spend 80 minutes on the first 60 questions. These questions often set the tone for the rest of the exam. Avoid overthinking and trust your preparation. - Dedicate another 80 minutes to questions 61-120. By now, you’re in the groove of answering questions, so maintain your pace. - Allocate 70 minutes for questions 121-180. This section requires speed and decisiveness as you’ll be approaching the end of the exam. Additionally: - Avoid the Panic Trap by taking deep breaths if feeling overwhelmed. - Skip overly complex questions and return to them later if needed. - Focus on maintaining a steady pace rather than striving for perfection.

Practical Tips to Prepare for Time Management

  • Take Timed Mock Tests: Gururo’s PMP Simulator provides a real exam-like experience to refine your timing.
  • Develop a Study Plan: Access Gururo’s free PMP application, simulator, and study plan to organize your preparation effectively.
  • Focus on Weak Areas: Identify question types that consume the most time and work on improving your speed.
  • Practice Mental Resilience: Use relaxation techniques such as deep breathing or visualization to stay calm under pressure.

Chapter 8: Sample Questions and Detailed Answers

During which process group is the project charter developed?

Initiating
The project charter is developed during the Initiating process group to formally authorize a project. This document gives the project manager the authority to apply organizational resources to project activities.

Which of the following is NOT a component of the project management plan?

D. Stakeholder Register
The correct answer is D. The Stakeholder Register is a supporting document and not a component of the project management plan. The project management plan includes various subsidiary plans like scope, schedule, and cost management plans.

What is the purpose of the Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)?

A. To define project tasks and activities in a hierarchical structure.
The WBS is a deliverable-oriented decomposition of a project into smaller components. It helps in organizing and defining the total scope of the project.

A project manager is calculating the Earned Value Management (EVM) metrics. The project has a planned value (PV) of $50,000, an earned value (EV) of $40,000, and an actual cost (AC) of $45,000. What is the Cost Performance Index (CPI)?

CPI = EV / AC = $40,000 / $45,000 ≈ 0.89
CPI indicates that the project is over budget because it shows that for every dollar earned, it costs approximately 1.11 dollars.

What does the term 'critical chain' refer to in project management?

The critical chain is the sequence of tasks that determines the minimum duration required to complete a project.
It helps in identifying bottlenecks and ensuring that resources are allocated efficiently.

How do you manage your time during the PMP exam according to Simplilearn?

Each question deserves approximately 1.2 minutes of your time. Track your progress to maintain a steady pace throughout the exam.
This method helps in managing time effectively by allocating equal time for each question and maintaining a buffer for difficult ones.

What does 'mark for review' mean in the context of PMP exam strategy?

Marking questions for review allows you to bookmark them and answer them at the end of the exam if needed.
This technique helps in managing time by focusing on easier questions first and saving harder ones for later.

How long should each section take according to Gururo's 80-80-70 rule?

The first section (questions 1-60) should take approximately 80 minutes. The second section (questions 61-120) should take another 80 minutes. The final section (questions 121-180) should take about 70 minutes.
This rule helps in dividing the exam into manageable chunks based on question difficulty and complexity.

What happens if you submit a set of questions during the PMP exam?

Once submitted, you cannot go back to those questions again.
This means that if you mark questions for review in one set, you'll need to answer them when you come back from your break or later in another set.

How does tracking progress help during the PMP exam?

Tracking progress helps by giving you an idea of how much time per question you can allow yourself for the rest of your exam.
It also distracts from worrying about passing or failing by focusing on maintaining a steady pace throughout.

What are some practical tips for managing mental resilience during exams?

Practical tips include using relaxation techniques such as deep breathing or visualization.
These techniques help in staying calm under pressure and maintaining focus throughout the exam.

How does identifying weak areas help in preparing for time management?

Identifying weak areas helps by focusing on improving speed in those areas where you consume most time.
It allows for targeted practice to optimize your performance during the actual exam, ensuring you don't get stuck on challenging question types.

A project manager is developing the project schedule. Which of the following is NOT a common tool or technique used in Schedule Management?

  1. A. Gantt Charts
  2. B. Critical Path Method (CPM)
  3. C. Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)
  4. D. Resource Leveling
C. Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)
The WBS is a key output of Scope Management, used to decompose project deliverables into smaller, manageable components. While it influences the schedule, it is not a direct tool/technique for Schedule Management itself. Gantt Charts, CPM, and Resource Leveling are all commonly used in Schedule Management.

During which process group would a project manager obtain formal acceptance of the project deliverables?

Closing
Formal acceptance of deliverables is a crucial activity in the Closing Process Group, signaling the completion of the project or phase.

A project team is facing scope creep. Which document would be most helpful in controlling this?

  1. A. Project Charter
  2. B. Stakeholder Register
  3. C. Scope Baseline
  4. D. Risk Register
C. Scope Baseline
The Scope Baseline, which includes the scope statement, WBS, and WBS dictionary, serves as the approved version of the project scope. Any deviation from it indicates scope creep and requires formal change control.

What is the primary output of the "Plan Risk Management" process?

Risk Management Plan
The Plan Risk Management process defines how to conduct risk management activities for a project. Its primary output is the Risk Management Plan, which details how risks will be identified, analyzed, responded to, and controlled.

In an Agile project, who is primarily responsible for prioritizing the backlog?

  1. A. Scrum Master
  2. B. Development Team
  3. C. Product Owner
  4. D. Project Sponsor
C. Product Owner
The Product Owner is responsible for maximizing the value of the product resulting from the work of the Development Team. This includes managing and prioritizing the Product Backlog.

Which of the following is a common tool for visualizing project progress in Agile, showing work remaining versus time?

Burndown Chart
A Burndown Chart is an essential Agile tool that visually represents the remaining work in a sprint or release against the time remaining.

When a project has a CPI of 1.2 and an SPI of 0.9, what does this indicate about the project's performance?

  1. A. Under budget, ahead of schedule
  2. B. Over budget, behind schedule
  3. C. Under budget, behind schedule
  4. D. Over budget, ahead of schedule
C. Under budget, behind schedule
A CPI > 1 indicates the project is under budget (cost-efficient). An SPI < 1 indicates the project is behind schedule (less work has been completed than planned).

What is the purpose of a change request in project management?

A formal proposal to modify any document, deliverable, or baseline.
Change requests are an integral part of integrated change control and are used to manage any modifications to the project.

Which knowledge area deals with optimizing the use of project resources, including team members, equipment, and materials?

Resource Management
Project Resource Management includes the processes to identify, acquire, and manage the resources needed for the successful completion of the project.

What is a key benefit of performing a "lessons learned" session at the end of a project?

To capture knowledge and insights for future projects and improve organizational processes.
Lessons learned are invaluable inputs to organizational process assets, helping the organization continuously improve its project management practices.

A stakeholder has a high interest and high power. What engagement strategy should the project manager use?

  1. A. Keep Informed
  2. B. Keep Satisfied
  3. C. Manage Closely
  4. D. Monitor
C. Manage Closely
For stakeholders with high power and high interest, the strategy is to manage them closely, ensuring their continuous engagement and satisfaction. This is crucial for project success.

What is the primary difference between a project and an operation?

Projects are temporary endeavors to create a unique product/service/result, while operations are ongoing, repetitive functions.
This fundamental distinction highlights the temporary and unique nature of projects versus the continuous and standardized nature of operations.

Which risk response strategy involves eliminating the threat entirely?

  1. A. Mitigate
  2. B. Accept
  3. C. Avoid
  4. D. Transfer
C. Avoid
Risk avoidance means changing the project plan to eliminate the threat entirely or to protect the project objectives from the risk's impact.

What is the "Cone of Uncertainty" in project management?

A concept that illustrates how the accuracy of project estimates improves over time as more information becomes known.
At the beginning of a project, the level of uncertainty is high, and estimates are broad. As the project progresses, uncertainty decreases, and estimates become more precise.

Which of the following is typically developed in the Planning Process Group?

  1. A. Project Charter
  2. B. Final Report
  3. C. Project Management Plan
  4. D. Accepted Deliverables
C. Project Management Plan
The Project Management Plan is the comprehensive document developed during Planning that details how the project will be executed, monitored, controlled, and closed.

What is the primary purpose of a Project Kick-off Meeting?

To formally introduce the project team, stakeholders, and align everyone on project goals and expectations.
The kick-off meeting marks the official start of the project and is vital for building team cohesion and shared understanding.

A project manager is performing a Pareto chart analysis. Which knowledge area is this tool primarily associated with?

  1. A. Cost Management
  2. B. Quality Management
  3. C. Risk Management
  4. D. Procurement Management
B. Quality Management
A Pareto chart is a specific type of bar chart, ordered by frequency, that shows which factors are the biggest contributors to a problem. It's a key tool in quality control for identifying the "vital few" causes that create the "trivial many" problems (80/20 rule).

What is the role of a Scrum Master in an Agile project?

Facilitates Scrum events, coaches the team, and removes impediments.
The Scrum Master is a servant-leader who helps the Scrum Team understand Scrum theory, practices, rules, and values, and ensures the team adheres to Scrum processes.

If your Schedule Performance Index (SPI) is 1.15, what does this mean for your project?

  1. A. The project is over budget.
  2. B. The project is behind schedule.
  3. C. The project is ahead of schedule.
  4. D. The project is on budget.
C. The project is ahead of schedule.
An SPI greater than 1.0 indicates that the project is ahead of the planned schedule. You are earning value faster than planned.

Which document formally authorizes the existence of a project and provides the project manager with the authority to apply organizational resources to project activities?

Project Charter
The Project Charter is a key output of the Initiating Process Group and is essential for formalizing the project and empowering the project manager.

A project manager is performing a "Make-or-Buy Analysis." This is typically associated with which knowledge area?

  1. A. Resource Management
  2. B. Cost Management
  3. C. Procurement Management
  4. D. Scope Management
C. Procurement Management
Make-or-Buy Analysis is a technique used in Plan Procurement Management to determine whether particular goods or services needed for a project should be produced internally or purchased from outside the organization.

What is the "Triple Constraint" of project management?

Scope, Time, and Cost
The Triple Constraint (or Iron Triangle) represents the three interdependent factors that influence the quality of a project: scope (or quality), time (schedule), and cost (budget). A change in one typically impacts the others.

In a matrix organization, who typically holds more power: the Functional Manager or the Project Manager?

  1. A. Functional Manager
  2. B. Project Manager
  3. C. Both have equal power
  4. D. It depends on the type of matrix organization.
D. It depends on the type of matrix organization.
In a strong matrix, the project manager has more power. In a balanced matrix, power is shared equally. In a weak matrix, the functional manager has more power.

What is the formula for Estimate At Completion (EAC) when the original budget is no longer valid, and you need a new estimate from scratch?

EAC = AC + Bottom-up ETC
This formula is used when past performance indicators suggest that the original estimate is flawed, and a new, detailed estimate for the remaining work (Bottom-up ETC) is required.

Which type of contract is most beneficial to the buyer, as the seller bears most of the risk?

  1. A. Cost-Plus-Fixed-Fee (CPFF)
  2. B. Fixed-Price (FP)
  3. C. Time and Material (T&M)
  4. D. Cost-Plus-Incentive-Fee (CPIF)
B. Fixed-Price (FP)
In a Fixed-Price contract, the seller agrees to provide the specified product or service at a fixed total price, regardless of the cost incurred. This puts the risk of cost overruns squarely on the seller.

What is the primary purpose of conducting a "Perform Quality Control" process?

To monitor and record results of executing the quality activities to assess performance and recommend necessary changes.
Quality Control focuses on identifying issues with deliverables and processes, ensuring they meet the required quality standards.

Which organizational structure categorizes projects by department, where project managers have little authority?

  1. A. Functional
  2. B. Projectized
  3. C. Matrix
  4. D. Balanced
A. Functional
In a functional organization, resources are grouped by specialty (e.g., engineering, marketing). Project managers typically have very limited authority, and teams report directly to their functional managers.

What is the primary purpose of a "Project Management Information System" (PMIS)?

To collect, integrate, and disseminate the outputs of all project management processes.
A PMIS is an information system consisting of the tools and techniques used to gather, integrate, and disseminate the outputs of project management processes. It can include automated tools, software, and manual systems.

During which process do project managers acquire team members, facilities, equipment, materials, and other resources necessary to complete project work?

  1. A. Estimate Resources
  2. B. Acquire Resources
  3. C. Develop Team
  4. D. Control Resources
B. Acquire Resources
The Acquire Resources process is part of the Executing Process Group within Resource Management, focusing on obtaining the resources needed for the project.

What is the "Gold Plating" in project management?

Adding extra features or functionality to the project deliverable that were not part of the original scope.
Gold plating is generally discouraged as it can lead to cost overruns, schedule delays, and may not align with customer needs. It's about doing more than what was asked.

Which type of chart is best for showing dependencies between project activities and identifying the critical path?

  1. A. Bar Chart (Gantt Chart)
  2. B. Histogram
  3. C. Network Diagram
  4. D. Run Chart
C. Network Diagram
Project network diagrams visually represent the sequence of project activities and their dependencies, which is crucial for determining the critical path.

What is the purpose of "Validate Scope" in project management?

To formalize acceptance of the completed project deliverables.
Validate Scope is the process of formalizing acceptance of the completed project deliverables. It involves reviewing deliverables with the customer or sponsor to ensure they are acceptable.

In Agile, what is a "Daily Scrum"?

A 15-minute time-boxed event for the Development Team to synchronize activities and create a plan for the next 24 hours.
The Daily Scrum is a key meeting in Scrum, fostering communication and allowing the team to assess progress towards the Sprint Goal.

Which knowledge area primarily focuses on how project information is generated, distributed, stored, and ultimately disposed of?

  1. A. Stakeholder Management
  2. B. Integration Management
  3. C. Communications Management
  4. D. Scope Management
C. Communications Management
Project Communications Management focuses on the processes required to ensure timely and appropriate planning, collection, creation, distribution, storage, retrieval, management, control, and ultimate disposition of project information.

A project manager is performing "Reserve Analysis." This is a tool/technique used in which process?

Estimate Costs (and Estimate Activity Durations)
Reserve Analysis involves adding contingency reserves (for known-unknowns) and management reserves (for unknown-unknowns) to estimates to account for cost and time uncertainty. It's used in both Estimate Costs and Estimate Activity Durations.

What is the primary output of the "Plan Quality Management" process?

  1. A. Quality Control Measurements
  2. B. Quality Metrics
  3. C. Quality Management Plan
  4. D. Verified Deliverables
C. Quality Management Plan
The Quality Management Plan is a component of the project management plan that describes how applicable policies, procedures, and guidelines will be implemented to achieve the quality objectives.

Which of the following describes a "Functional Organization"?

An organizational structure where personnel are grouped by department or functional area, and project managers have limited authority.
In a functional organization, resources are typically owned and controlled by functional managers, leading to weak project manager authority.

What is the main goal of "Develop Project Team" process?

To improve competencies, team interaction, and the overall team environment to enhance project performance.
This process focuses on developing the individual and collective skills of the project team members, leading to better collaboration and project outcomes.

What is a "Contingency Reserve"?

Time or money allocated in the schedule or cost baseline for identified risks (known-unknowns).
Contingency reserves are for risks that have been identified and planned for, whereas management reserves are for unknown-unknowns.

Which process group involves defining a new project or a new phase of an existing project by obtaining authorization to start the project or phase?

  1. A. Planning
  2. B. Executing
  3. C. Initiating
  4. D. Closing
C. Initiating
The Initiating Process Group focuses on authorizing the project or its next phase and defining initial stakeholders.

What is "Crashing" in schedule compression techniques?

Adding resources to shorten the project duration for the least incremental cost.
Crashing focuses on activities on the critical path, aiming to reduce their duration by adding more resources, always considering the cost-effectiveness.

Which of the following is an Enterprise Environmental Factor (EEF)?

  1. A. Lessons Learned Repository
  2. B. Organizational Culture
  3. C. Project Management Plan
  4. D. WBS Template
B. Organizational Culture
EEFs are conditions, not under the control of the project team, that influence, constrain, or direct the project. Organizational culture is an internal EEF. OPAs (Organizational Process Assets) include lessons learned and WBS templates.

What is the "Definition of Done" in Scrum?

A shared understanding of what it means for work to be complete, adhering to quality standards.
The Definition of Done ensures transparency and provides a common reference point for the Development Team regarding the quality and completeness of Increment features.

If your Cost Performance Index (CPI) is 0.85, what does this indicate about your project?

  1. A. The project is under budget.
  2. B. The project is over budget.
  3. C. The project is on budget.
  4. D. The project is ahead of schedule.
B. The project is over budget.
A CPI less than 1.0 indicates that the project is over budget. For every dollar earned, you are spending more than a dollar.

What is the primary output of the "Plan Communications Management" process?

Communications Management Plan
This plan details how, when, and by whom project information will be administered and distributed.

When should a project manager use a "Bottom-Up Estimating" technique?

  1. A. When there is limited detailed information.
  2. B. When a high-level estimate is sufficient.
  3. C. When high accuracy is required and detailed work is known.
  4. D. For quick, early-stage estimates.
C. When high accuracy is required and detailed work is known.
Bottom-Up Estimating involves estimating individual work packages or activities with a high degree of detail and then aggregating these estimates to arrive at a total project estimate. It's time-consuming but highly accurate.

What is "Forecasting" in project management?

Estimating future project conditions and events based on information and knowledge available at the time of the forecast.
Forecasting involves predicting future project performance based on current performance and trends, often using EVM metrics (e.g., EAC).

Which type of communication is most effective for complex or sensitive information?

  1. A. Email
  2. B. Formal written report
  3. C. Face-to-face meeting
  4. D. Group chat
C. Face-to-face meeting
Face-to-face communication offers the highest bandwidth, allowing for immediate feedback, observation of non-verbal cues, and clarification, which is critical for complex or sensitive discussions.

What is the objective of "Qualitative Risk Analysis"?

To prioritize individual project risks for further analysis or action by assessing their probability of occurrence and impact.
Qualitative risk analysis helps in focusing resources on the most critical risks, often using tools like probability and impact matrix.

What is a "Lag" in project scheduling?

  1. A. The amount of time an activity can be delayed without delaying the project finish date.
  2. B. The amount of time an activity can be accelerated.
  3. C. A mandatory waiting period between activities.
  4. D. The time an activity takes to complete.
C. A mandatory waiting period between activities.
A lag is a modification of a logical relationship that directs a delay in the successor activity. For example, "start-to-finish with a 5-day lag" means the successor activity can finish 5 days after the predecessor starts.

Which is a tool or technique for "Control Quality"?

Statistical Sampling
Statistical sampling involves choosing a subset of a population for inspection. It's often used when inspecting every item is impractical, and it's a key technique in Quality Control. Other tools include control charts, inspections, and flowcharts.

What is the difference between validation and verification?

Validation is "Are we building the right product?", while verification is "Are we building the product right?".
Verification (Control Quality) ensures the product meets specifications. Validation (Validate Scope) ensures the product meets user/stakeholder needs.

What is the purpose of a "Sprint Review" in Scrum?

  1. A. To plan the next Sprint.
  2. B. To inspect the Increment and adapt the Product Backlog if needed.
  3. C. To inspect how the last Sprint went regarding individuals, interactions, processes, tools, and their Definition of Done.
  4. D. To assign tasks to team members.
B. To inspect the Increment and adapt the Product Backlog if needed.
The Sprint Review is held at the end of the Sprint to inspect the Increment and adapt the Product Backlog if needed. It's an informal meeting, not a status meeting.

Which of the following is typically NOT included in the Project Charter?

  1. A. Project purpose or justification
  2. B. Detailed WBS
  3. C. High-level requirements
  4. D. Project Manager's assigned authority
B. Detailed WBS
The Project Charter is a high-level document. The detailed WBS is developed during the Planning Process Group as part of Scope Management.

What is the purpose of a "Bidder Conference"?

To clarify procurement documents and answer questions from potential sellers.
Bidder conferences (also called pre-bid conferences or contractor conferences) are meetings between the buyer and prospective sellers prior to submittal of a bid or proposal. They ensure all prospective sellers have a clear and common understanding of the procurement.

A project manager is facing a risk with a high probability and high impact. Which risk response strategy would be most appropriate?

  1. A. Accept
  2. B. Exploit
  3. C. Avoid or Mitigate
  4. D. Share
C. Avoid or Mitigate
For high-probability, high-impact threats, the project manager should actively work to avoid the risk or mitigate its impact and/or probability to an acceptable level.

What is "Analogous Estimating"?

A top-down estimating technique that uses parameters from a previous, similar project, such as duration, budget, and size, as the basis for estimating the current project.
Analogous estimating is less accurate but quick, often used in early project phases when detailed information is limited.

Which of the following is an input to the "Develop Project Management Plan" process?

  1. A. Deliverables
  2. B. Approved Change Requests
  3. C. Project Charter
  4. D. Work Performance Information
C. Project Charter
The Project Charter provides the initial project information and authorization needed to begin the detailed planning activities, leading to the Project Management Plan.

What is the main purpose of "Project Baselines" (scope, schedule, cost)?

To establish a reference point against which project performance is measured.
Baselines are fixed, approved versions of the project plan that serve as a comparison for actual performance. Any changes to baselines require formal change control.

Which of the following describes a "Projectized Organization"?

  2. B. Team members report to both a functional and project manager.
  3. C. Team members are typically co-located and report directly to the project manager.
  4. D. Power is balanced between functional and project managers.
C. Team members are typically co-located and report directly to the project manager.
In a projectized organization, the project manager has high authority, and resources are often dedicated to the project.

What is the "Rolling Wave Planning" technique?

An iterative planning technique where the work to be accomplished in the near term is planned in detail, while the work further in the future is planned at a higher level.
Rolling Wave Planning is especially useful in Agile or projects with high uncertainty, allowing for progressive elaboration.

What is a "Control Chart" used for in Quality Management?

  1. A. To show the sequence of activities.
  2. B. To track stakeholder engagement levels.
  3. C. To determine if a process is in or out of statistical control.
  4. D. To prioritize risks.
C. To determine if a process is in or out of statistical control.
Control charts graphically display process data over time, with upper and lower control limits. Points outside these limits or non-random patterns indicate that the process is out of control.

What is a "Risk Register"?

A document in which the results of risk analysis and risk response planning are recorded.
The Risk Register is a key output of several risk management processes, serving as a comprehensive list of identified risks, their potential impacts, and planned responses.

In Agile, what is a "Sprint Backlog"?

Which of the following is a common input to most project management processes?

  2. B. Deliverables
  3. C. Change Requests
  4. D. Accepted Deliverables

What is the purpose of a "Sprint Retrospective" in Scrum?

To plan ways to increase quality and effectiveness by inspecting how the last Sprint went and identifying improvements.
The Sprint Retrospective is an opportunity for the Scrum Team to inspect itself and create a plan for improvements to be enacted during the next Sprint.

What is the "Rule of Seven" in control charts?

If seven data points in a row are all above or all below the mean, or are all increasing or all decreasing, it indicates a non-random pattern and the process is out of control.
Even if all points are within the control limits, a "run of seven" indicates a systematic cause is affecting the process, warranting investigation.

  3. C. Control Procurements
  4. D. Close Procurements
B. Conduct Procurements
Conduct Procurements is the process of obtaining seller responses, selecting a seller, and awarding a contract.

What is a "Project Sponsor"?

The person or group who provides resources and support for the project and is accountable for enabling success.
The project sponsor plays a critical role in project initiation and ongoing support, acting as a high-level advocate for the project.

What is the primary characteristic of "Parametric Estimating"?

  1. A. Expert judgment based on past experience.
  2. B. Uses a statistical relationship between historical data and other variables to calculate an estimate.
  3. C. Involves breaking down work into smaller components and estimating each.
  4. D. Estimates are provided by external vendors.
B. Uses a statistical relationship between historical data and other variables to calculate an estimate.
Parametric estimating uses a unit rate (e.g., cost per line of code, cost per square foot) multiplied by the quantity of work to be performed. It is more accurate than analogous estimating.

Which of the following is an example of a "Preventive Action"?

  1. A. Reworking a defective component.
  2. B. Adjusting project schedule to avoid a potential delay.
  3. C. Repairing a bug in software.
  4. D. Correcting a budget overrun after it occurred.
B. Adjusting project schedule to avoid a potential delay.
Preventive actions are taken to reduce the probability of a negative impact from a risk event. Corrective actions fix issues after they occur.

What is the primary focus of "Manage Communications" process?

To ensure timely and appropriate collection, creation, distribution, storage, retrieval, management, control, and disposition of project information.
This process focuses on the actual execution of the communications plan, ensuring information flows effectively to all stakeholders.

What is the formula for the Three-Point Estimate (Triangular Distribution)?

E = (O + M + P) / 3
Where O = Optimistic, M = Most Likely, and P = Pessimistic. This formula is often used for activity duration or cost estimation when there is uncertainty.

What is a "Product Backlog" in Scrum?

  4. D. A document defining the project scope baseline.
B. An ordered list of everything that might be needed in the product.
The Product Backlog is dynamic, ordered by the Product Owner, and serves as the single source of requirements for any changes to be made to the product.

What is "Risk Transference"?

Shifting the impact of a threat along with ownership of the response to a third party.

What is the primary difference between a Program and a Portfolio?

A Program is a group of related projects managed to gain benefits not available from managing them individually, while a Portfolio is a collection of projects, programs, and operations aligned with strategic objectives.
Programs are about coordinated management for synergistic benefits, whereas portfolios are about achieving strategic business goals through a collection of diverse initiatives.

What is the "Theory of Constraints"?

A management paradigm that states that any "system" (e.g., a project) is always limited by a small number of constraints, and to improve the system, the constraint must be identified and managed.
This theory is closely related to the Critical Chain Method, which focuses on managing the most significant constraints in a project.

User stories are a primary way to capture requirements in Agile, focusing on value and allowing for flexibility.
